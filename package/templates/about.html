HOW A COFFEE ROASTER WORKS

A simple drum coffee roaster consists of a cylindrical drum that rotates on a horizontal axis. The roasting process begins by preheating the drum. Green coffee beans are then loaded into the drum, and the roasting begins. As the drum rotates, hot air is circulated around the beans, evenly heating them. The heat source, typically a gas burner or electric heating element, provides the necessary thermal energy to roast the beans. The temperature inside the drum is controlled to ensure proper roasting. During the roasting process, the beans undergo various chemical and physical changes, resulting in the development of desirable flavors and aromas. The roaster operator monitors the roasting progress by observing the color and aroma of the beans. The roasting time and temperature can be adjusted to achieve the desired roast level. After reaching the desired roast, the beans are quickly cooled to halt the roasting process and preserve the flavor. Overall, the drum roaster provides a controlled environment for roasting coffee beans, allowing for precise temperature control and consistent results.
